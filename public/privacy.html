What Are Cookies?

Cookies are tiny text files that can be stored by your browser on your Internet-enabled device – computer, tablet or smartphone (“device”) – when you visit a website, subject to your consent. Some are session cookies, used only in the current browser session, while others are persistent cookies, which remain on your device and can be read again on subsequent visits to the website. This allows the website to "remember" your actions or preferences over time, for example, to log you in again on a subsequent visit.

A cookie will typically contain the name of the originating domain, the lifetime of the cookie (i.e. how long it will remain on your device), and a value, which is usually a randomly generated unique number.

Technologies from third-party providers which we integrate on our websites may set cookies on your device. These cookies identify you as a unique user by means of a tracking ID. The cookie itself is anonymous and does not contain any Personal Data, except when such information has been supplied by you, such as when you provide your name and email address when completing a registration form.

How do I reject or delete cookies?

Most internet browsers are initially set up to automatically support cookies. However, you can change your browser settings to decline all cookies, certain types of cookies, or specific cookies. You can also set your browser to alert you and request permission when cookies are being sent to your device. Further, you can delete stored cookies at any time. If you use different devices to view and access our website, you may need to ensure that each browser on each device is adjusted to suit your cookie preferences.

On entering our website, you will be offered a choice of whether to accept or reject associated cookies. If you refuse to accept cookies from us, you will still be able to visit our website, but some of the functions may not work correctly. By using our website without deleting or rejecting some or all cookies, you will be deemed to consent to the placement of cookies on any device used to access our site.
